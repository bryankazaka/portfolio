About Me

Beginning at a small school in South Africa, my journey was propelled forward by an Allan Gray Orbis Foundation Bursary to the reknown Bishops Diocesan College. This path led me to a full-ride university scholarship through the Jakes Gerwel Fellowship to the top tertiary instution in Africa, The University of Cape Town, where I majored in Computer Science and Information Systems, eventually honing my focus on Computer Science. My academic pursuits have been complemented by a deep engagement in research, particularly within the realms of Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence, contributing to my goal of enhancing collective scientific knowledge in these fields.

When I am not working, you can often find me reading, as an avid reader of philosophy and murder mysteries, with Agatha Christie standing out as my favorite author. Bodybuilding is also a key part of my daily routine, with regular workouts at the gym. Additionally, I've dedicated myself to studying Japanese for two years, aiming to immerse myself in its culture and potentially live there one day.
